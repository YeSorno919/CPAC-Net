

# ⭐Complementary Pseudo-label and Augmentation Consistency Network

This is the second major research content in my dissertation.

## 💡 Introduction

**📝Abstract摘要**

  To address the issues of limited registration generalization and high contrastive learning complexity in [existing meibomian gland segmentation methods](https://github.com/YeSorno919/SS-MGS), this paper further proposes a semi-supervised meibomian gland segmentation network named Complementary Pseudo-label and Augmentation Consistency Network (**CPAC-Net**). Without relying on complex registration sampling pipelines, this method constructs a dual-source complementary pseudo-label fusion mechanism, which fuses low-uncertainty regions from the Mean Teacher and high-confidence regions mined by intra-patient image registration to generate high-quality pseudo-labels. Meanwhile, deformed image supplementary supervision is introduced to enrich supervisory signals, and a consistency scheme of dynamic random strong augmentation and probabilistically independent weak augmentation is designed to guide the model in learning robust and discriminative feature representations. The innovations of this paper are as follows:

  (1) A dual-source complementary pseudo-label fusion mechanism (**DCPF**) is proposed, which realizes label propagation through intra-patient registration, fuses low-uncertainty regions from the Mean Teacher and high-confidence regions mined by registration to generate high-quality pseudo-labels, alleviates the information limitation of a single pseudo-label source, and breaks through the information bottleneck of traditional pseudo-label generation.

  (2) Deformed image supplementary supervision **(DISS**) is introduced, which uses semantically consistent deformed samples generated by registration to expand effective supervisory signals, effectively improving the model’s adaptability to morphological variations of meibomian glands and generalization performance, and enriching the dimension of supervisory information.

  (3) A dynamic random strong augmentation-probabilistically independent weak augmentation consistency method (**DRS-PIWAC**) is designed. It explores the diverse feature distribution of data through dynamic random strong augmentation, and combines the constraint of prediction consistency from probabilistically independent weak augmentation to guide the model in learning more robust feature representations, significantly enhancing the model’s generalization ability in complex clinical scenarios.

  Experiments demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms the fully supervised U-Net at a 25% annotation ratio and surpasses mainstream semi-supervised segmentation methods under all annotation settings, while achieving promising performance in clinical scenario simulations. Compared with previous methods, the proposed approach maintains low inference cost, improves training efficiency, reduces data dependency, and achieves better segmentation accuracy and stability, which further enhances its potential for clinical applications and provides a novel paradigm for the integration of registration techniques and semi-supervised meibomian gland segmentation.

## 🏕️ Dataset
  The dataset, provided by the Ophthalmology Department of the Fujian Provincial Hospital, includes 292 infrared images of upper eyelid meibomian glands, collected from 73 MGD patients( 4 images per patient) with a resolution of
740 × 350 pixels. All images were pixel-level annotated by ophthalmologists. The dataset was then split into training, validation, and test sets in a 7:1:2 ratio. Each patient’s data was strictly limited to appear in just one set.
